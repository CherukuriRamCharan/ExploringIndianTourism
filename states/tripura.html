Top 10 Places to Visit in Tripura

Ujjayanta Palace

A stunning palace-turned-museum located in Agartala.

Read moreBuilt by Maharaja Radha Kishore Manikya, it showcases Tripura's royal heritage and culture.

Neermahal

The only water palace in Eastern India located in Rudrasagar Lake.

Read moreA mix of Hindu and Mughal architecture, it offers boat rides and sunset views.

Unakoti

Ancient rock carvings and Shaivite sculptures carved into a hill.

Read moreThese massive 7th–9th century carvings are surrounded by lush forests, ideal for trekking and photography.

Tripura Sundari Temple

One of the 51 Shakti Peethas, located in Udaipur, Tripura.

Read moreThis red-and-white temple is a spiritual hub, especially during Diwali's annual fair.

Jampui Hills

A serene hill station known for oranges and panoramic views.

Read moreIdeal for trekking, the hills offer beautiful sunrises and a peaceful escape into nature.

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

A biodiversity hotspot with lakes, boating, and an impressive zoo.

Read moreHome to clouded leopards, it’s perfect for birdwatchers, researchers, and families.

Bhuvaneswari Temple

A beautiful riverside temple with historical and literary significance.

Read moreLocated near the Gomati River, it inspired works by Rabindranath Tagore.

Gunabati Group of Temples

A trio of 17th-century temples reflecting Tripura’s rich religious architecture.

Read moreThese ancient shrines in Udaipur are known for their terracotta-style artistry and calm surroundings.

Chabimura

Famous for rock carvings on the steep mountain walls beside a river.

Read moreTake a boat ride to see massive stone images of deities along the Gomati river cliffs.

Akhaura Check Post

A cross-border point that connects Agartala to Bangladesh’s Akhaura.

Read moreOffers cultural exchange opportunities and an Indo-Bangla border ceremony similar to Wagah.
